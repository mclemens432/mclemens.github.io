2024 Sewing Gallery

Hi! I'm Melissa, and I love to sew. 2024 has been a productive sewing year for me so far, so I thought I'd share my portfolio of projects with some information about the drafting & construction process. Click to jump to any particular piece:

Pencil Skirt   Festival Top   Graduation dress   Lightweight button-down   Maxi Dress   Monster Apron   Kimono

  1. Pencil Skirt

    • This was my first project of the year. I love this luxe fabric, although the end result turned out a little fancier than I'd expected!
    • Fabric: jacquard knit
    • Pattern: self-drafted using this tutorial

  2. Festival Top

    • As soon as we bought tickets to the Celtic Festival in Grass Valley, I was hit with the inspiration for this fun and flirty top. The fabric was a dream to work with, and I made my own bias tape for the first time - a process that I've brought to several other projects this year.
    • Fabric: rayon/linen blend
    • Pattern: self-drafted by tracing & altering existing patterns

  3. Graduation Dress

    • I'd been planning this project for a while, and I am mostly in love with how it came out. I wish that the lace had more drape, but I think it's still a stylish and flattering dress.
    • Fabric: polyester lace & polyester or poly/cotton lining
    • Pattern: Simplicity 7082, a vintage 1967 pattern

  4. Lightweight Button-down

    • I was so happy with this project! I finally made it after buying the fabric & conceiving of the design a year earlier. The shirt is perfect for those hot summer days.
    • Fabric: cotton gauze
    • Pattern: self-drafted by tracing & altering an existing shirt

  5. Maxi Dress

    • This was a quick & satisfying project that involved overcoming a tragic mistake: I accidentally cut a large slit in the skirt while trimming the seam allowance. It took some extra trimming and seam ripping, but the dress turned out great.
    • Fabric: pre-smocked rayon
    • Pattern: self-drafted, but referenced this tutorial from Joann.

  6. Monster Apron

    • I whipped this up in a single morning after my son dug the fabric out of a giveaway bag. I'm so happy with how it turned out - and so is he!
    • Fabric: quilting cotton
    • Pattern: tweaked this tutorial by adding a facing fabric to both the apron and the pocket.

  7. Kimono

    • Repeat after me: Always. Make. A. Muslin. That would have helped me avoid the unfortunate first draft of this piece, which turned out absolutely huge. Luckily, I had lots of this fabric, so I took several inches off both length and width to create this more flattering silhouette.
    • Fabric: lightweight wool
    • Pattern: New Look 6378, heavily downsized!
